About

Our Story

Alex Dangel and Caitlin Toner founded NÜDL in the summer of 2015 with the overarching goal of improving our food system. Our passion for cooking and conversations transformed NÜDL into a meal-sharing network. We believe that people are the key to changing our food system. Since late July, we have brought together hundreds of Macalester students at 100 dinners and counting.

Philosophy

Food matters. So do people. Sharing home-cooked meals is healthy for the brain and the body. NÜDL believes that bringing people together to eat real food leads to deeper conversations about the communities we live in. 

Why are we doing this?

With universal mobile technology, we can bring a community together to share home-made meals and continue the move away from convenient, but unhealthy processed foods. Consumer data show that there has been a general trend away from processed foods towards the periphery of the grocery store (produce, grains, nuts, the "good" stuff") over the past 5 years. NÜDL is operating at the intersection of the movement against processed foods and the indisputable desire for authentic social interaction. 

How does it work?

NÜDL connects Guests with Hosts who cook. 

Guests: Sign up for a Nüdl, show up, and eat!

Hosts: Set the date, get groceries, and prepare the meal.

Anyone in the Macalester network is welcome to join the NÜDL community and to attend any Mealshare. Log in with your @macalester.edu email address for access to our meal-sharing network.

Interested in starting a NÜDL community outside of Macalester? Contact us!
